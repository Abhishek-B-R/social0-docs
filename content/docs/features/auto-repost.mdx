## Overview

Auto-repost features let you **resurface** posts (re-post or boost them on a schedule) and **auto-plug** (automatically add a plug or comment to posts that do well). Both are available on **Growth** and **Pro** plans. If you’re on Starter or Free, you won’t see these options until you upgrade.

## What is resurface?

Resurface lets you take a post you’ve already published and send it again (or boost it) on a schedule. For example, you publish a tweet today and set it to resurface next week so more people see it. You configure this when creating or editing a post, or from the post’s detail page.

## How to use resurface

1. Create a post (or open one you’ve already published).
2. Look for **Resurface** or **Re-post later** (wording may vary). Turn it on.
3. Set when you want it to go out again (date and time, or a queue slot).
4. Save or publish. When the time comes, Social0 will post it again to the accounts you chose.

You can also set resurface from the post detail page: open the post and use the resurface option there.

## What is auto-plug?

Auto-plug automatically adds a plug or comment to posts that perform well (e.g. get a lot of engagement). You turn it on in settings or per post if the option is offered. Social0 then applies the plug when the post meets the criteria you set.

## Where you’ll see these options

- When **creating** or **editing** a post, if your plan allows it you’ll see options for resurface and/or auto-plug.
- On the **post detail** page, you may see **Resurface** or **Post again** to send the same content later.

If you don’t see them, you’re likely on Starter or Free. Go to **Dashboard** → **Billing** to upgrade to Growth or Pro.

## Tips

- Use resurface for your best posts so they get a second (or third) run.
- Auto-plug is optional; turn it off if you prefer to add plugs yourself.

## Common questions

**Q: I don’t see Resurface or Auto-plug. Why?**  
A: They’re included on Growth and Pro plans. Upgrade from **Dashboard** → **Billing** to get them.

**Q: Can I choose which posts get auto-plug?**  
A: It depends on the product. If there’s a per-post toggle, you can. Otherwise it may apply to posts that meet certain performance rules.
