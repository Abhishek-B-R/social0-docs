## Overview

The **posting queue** is a set of weekly time slots in your timezone (e.g. 09:00 and 12:00 on chosen days). When you schedule a post and choose **Next Queue Slot** in the schedule panel, Social0 assigns it to the next available slot. You don’t pick the exact time-the queue does it. Posts assigned to a slot show as **Queued** in your Posts list (they’re still “scheduled”; the label just shows they’re in the queue).

**Important:** Editing your queue (adding, removing, or changing slots or days) does **not** change posts that are already scheduled. Only new “Next Queue Slot” choices use the updated schedule.

## Where to find it

- **Dashboard** → **Settings** → **Queue** tab (the page is titled **Posting queue**).

## How the queue is set up

The queue page shows a table:

- **Time** - Each row is one posting time (e.g. 09:00, 12:00). Times are in your timezone.
- **Days (Mon–Sun)** - For each time, you turn that slot on or off per weekday. A green check means the slot is active that day; an empty circle means it’s off.
- **+ Add posting time** - Adds another time row. You can have multiple times (e.g. morning and afternoon).
- **Remove (×)** - Use the × on a row to remove that posting time.

Your timezone is shown at the top (e.g. **Timezone: Asia/Calcutta**). All times in the table use that timezone.

## How to schedule a post into the queue

1. Create your post (Composer or Create flow) and select the accounts.
2. In the **Schedule post** section, turn scheduling on.
3. Choose **Next Queue Slot**. The UI shows the next free slot (e.g. “Sunday, Mar 15 – 12:00 PM”). That’s when the post will go out.
4. Click **Schedule**. The post is queued and will publish at that slot.

You can still schedule to a **specific date and time** instead-pick the date and time fields instead of using Next Queue Slot. Those posts are “scheduled” but not “queued” (they don’t use the queue slots).

## Where queue appears

- **Settings** → **Queue** - Create and edit your posting times and days.
- **Schedule panel** (when creating/scheduling a post) - **Next Queue Slot** uses these slots.
- **Posts list** - Queued posts show a **Queued** label and slot info.

## Tips

- Use a few consistent times (e.g. 09:00 and 12:00) on the days you want to post so the queue is predictable.
- If you don’t see **Next Queue Slot**, you haven’t added any posting times yet. Add at least one time (and at least one day) in **Settings** → **Queue**.

## Common questions

**What’s the difference between “Schedule” and “Queue”?**  
**Schedule** = you pick a specific date and time. **Queue** = you choose **Next Queue Slot** and the post is assigned to the next free slot from your queue. Both are scheduled; “Queued” just means it’s tied to a queue slot.

**If I change my queue, do existing scheduled posts change?**  
No. Editing your queue (times or days) does not affect posts that are already scheduled. Only new posts that you assign with **Next Queue Slot** use the updated queue.

**Can I change which slot a post is in?**  
Yes. Open the post from the Posts list and change its schedule (e.g. switch to a different time or to a different queue slot if you edit and re-schedule).
