## Overview

The queue lets you define time slots (e.g. “weekdays at 9 AM” or “every day at 6 PM”) and assign scheduled posts to those slots. Instead of picking an exact time for each post, you choose a slot and Social0 publishes the post in that window. Posts that are in a queue show as **Queued** in your Posts list.

## How to set up your queue

1. Go to **Dashboard** → **Settings**.
2. Open the **Queue** tab.
3. Add or edit **slots**—each slot is a day and time (or recurring pattern). For example: “Every weekday at 9:00 AM.”
4. Save. Your slots are now available when you schedule a post.

## How to schedule a post into the queue

1. Create your post and choose **Schedule**.
2. When picking the time, choose **Add to queue** (or the equivalent option) and select one of your slots.
3. Confirm. The post is queued for that slot and will be published in that time window.

You’ll see it in **Posts** with a **Queued** badge and which slot it’s in.

## Where queue appears

- **Settings** → **Queue** — Create and edit your slots.
- **Create/Schedule flow** — When scheduling, you can assign a post to a slot.
- **Posts list** — Queued posts show a **Queued** label and slot info.

## Tips

- Use a few consistent slots (e.g. morning and evening) so your audience knows when to expect posts.
- You can still schedule posts to a specific date and time without using the queue.

## Common questions

**Q: What’s the difference between “Schedule” and “Queue”?**  
A: **Schedule** = one specific date and time. **Queue** = one of your recurring slots. Both are “scheduled”; the queue just groups them into time windows you define.

**Q: Can I change which slot a post is in?**  
A: Yes. Open the post from the Posts list and change its schedule or queue assignment.
